Creating History

May 30th 2020 SpaceX succesfully launched Crew Dragon with two NASA astronauts into orbit from American soil, for the first time in nearly a decade. The next day the spacecraft autonomously docked to the International Space Station. With the astronauts Bob Behnken and Doug Hurley on board SpaceX returned human spaceflight to the United States. Being the first private company to send a Spacecraft to the International Space Station.

2002-2007

SpaceX is an american aerospace manufacturer and space transportation services company founded by CEO, Elon Musk. This early period is characterized by funding and testing.

2008-2017

This period is described as the Unmanned spaceflight period. In 2010 SpaceX introduced the SpaceX Dragon, and becomes the first commercially built and operated spacecraft to be recovered successfully from orbit. Crew Dragon gets revealed in 2014. In 2017 SpaceX achieved one of its great ambitions; the recovery and reuse of rockets.

2018-2019

SpaceX begins launching Falcon Heavy, the world most powerful operational rocket. In 2019 SpaceX´s next generation spacecraft, Starship a orbital prototype, begins initial-scaletests with success.

In 2019 SpaceX developed a second version of the Dragon called Crew Dragon or SpaceX Dragon 2, which includes the capability to transport people. Flight testing was completed in 2019.

2020

May 30th Crew Dragon successfully launched into orbit with the two NASA astronauts Bob Behnken and Doug Hurley on board. The next morning Crew Dragon autonomously docked with the International Space Station. This marks a milestone for SpaceX´s first crewed mission.

The Dragon is named by Musk, after the 1963 song "Puff, the Magic Dragon" by Peter, Paul and Mary.
